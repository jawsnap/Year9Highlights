By the Numbers

Like most years, Year 9 contains 365 photos (leap years have 366).
Here are some more quantitative ways to view the year.

  • 315+50

    By far most - 86%! - of my photos were taken with my (iPhone 6S). A mere 14% were taken with my (Nikon D600).

  • 336

    I greatly prefer landscape orientation, coming in at 92%. Only 29 photographs were portrait-oriented or square.

  • 26

    Photos that include my 2-year-old daughter. Don't worry, I've taken hundreds more, they're just not part of the One/Day Project :)

  • 3288

    The number of total photos taken at the end of Year 9. I started on 2/29/2008.

Favorites

I've selected twenty of my favorite photos from the year, including why I like them.
Click on the title of the photo to see it with all the others on www.jawsnap.net.

#300 | Christmas Ride

Somehow, this scene just worked perfectly. I still have trouble believing I got this lens flare with my iPhone.
Also, fashion.

#261 | Spin

I occasionally, perhaps often, take a sub-par photo and combine it with itself in Photoshop to create something new and interesting. I almost didn't do that with this photo, but I'm glad I did - I really like the sense of motion in the result.

#77 | Three Thousand

My mind is a little boggled that I've done this more than three thousand days in a row. Will I make it to four thousand? There's always another milestone coming up.

#140 | Energy

I like how this turned out, particularly because it's a single exposure. The light is a little blinking ball that turns on when you hit it - it was originally inside of a rubbery toy, but the rubber got sticky and gross, so I cut it open to get the ball out. For the photo, I 'froze' my hand with the flash, and then spun the blinking ball around in my hands for the long exposure. I can't remember for sure, but I'm pretty sure I dropped the ball into my hands from my mouth - it wasn't in my hands when the flash went off.

#234 | Gold Medal Flour

This is a scene from a conference I attended in Minneapolis. I was mainly in the shiny new Guthrie Theater, directly across from the ruins of the Gold Medal Flour mill. Guthrie Theater had a bunch of windows lined with mirrors, so this is a single exposure. I spent a good deal of time taking photos around these buildings. I remember going back for this photo - my first attempt wasn't quite spot-on. Usually the moment is lost if you miss it, but thankfully the buildings didn't go anywhere.
